Overall Strategy
Daily (Weekdays): 1 hour max per day.
Weekends: 2 hours max.
Cheat Days: 1-2 days per week where you can take time off from the plan.
Flexibility: The schedule allows you to adjust focus areas weekly. Tasks are spread over a period, keeping the workload light.
Tracking Progress: Use a simple task management tool (like Trello, Todoist, or a notebook) to track weekly goals and mark completed tasks.
Week 1 to Week 4: Initial Focus on Cloud-Native Architecture & DevOps
Goal: Lay a solid foundation in cloud-native architecture and build DevOps knowledge.
Weekdays (1 Hour per Day)
Day 1 (Cloud-Native Architecture): Learn Kubernetes basics (30 mins reading, 30 mins practicing with Minikube/Docker).
Day 2 (DevOps): Learn Jenkins basics; Set up a basic pipeline (30 mins reading, 30 mins practice).
Day 3 (Cloud-Native Architecture): Study AWS services (e.g., EC2, Lambda) for cloud-native applications (1 hour).
Day 4 (DevOps): Practice containerization with Docker (30 mins video, 30 mins hands-on).
Day 5 (Leadership & Strategy): Read an article/book on leadership strategies (1 hour).
Weekend (2 Hours)
Day 6 (Cloud-Native + DevOps): Work on setting up a basic CI/CD pipeline with Jenkins and Docker (2 hours total).
Day 7 (Cheat Day): No work/study.
Tracking:
End of Week 1-4: Create small checkpoints (like completing a Jenkins pipeline setup or deploying a basic app in Kubernetes). Track progress through task management software by marking off completed tasks.
Week 5 to Week 8: Focus on Microservices & CI/CD
Goal: Deepen microservices knowledge and strengthen CI/CD expertise.
Weekdays (1 Hour per Day)
Day 1 (Microservices): Learn about microservices design patterns (30 mins reading, 30 mins diagramming).
Day 2 (CI/CD): Automate testing in Jenkins CI/CD pipeline (1 hour).
Day 3 (Microservices): Study Spring Cloud and Netflix OSS libraries (1 hour).
Day 4 (Microservices): Work on a microservices design for a real-world case study (30 mins theory, 30 mins hands-on).
Day 5 (Leadership & Mentorship): Watch a leadership video or listen to a podcast (1 hour).
Weekend (2 Hours)
Day 6 (Microservices + CI/CD): Implement a simple microservices app with a CI/CD pipeline (2 hours).
Day 7 (Cheat Day): Take a break.
Tracking:
End of Week 5-8: Achieve a milestone like deploying a microservice with continuous delivery. Track weekly deliverables like completing Jenkins automations or implementing microservices communication.
Week 9 to Week 12: Focus on AI/ML Integration & Public Speaking
Goal: Explore AI/ML concepts and refine public speaking/thought leadership skills.
Weekdays (1 Hour per Day)
Day 1 (AI/ML Basics): Learn the basics of machine learning models (1 hour).
Day 2 (Public Speaking): Write a blog post or practice a short presentation (1 hour).
Day 3 (AI/ML + Cloud): Explore machine learning in AWS SageMaker or Google Cloud AI (1 hour).
Day 4 (Public Speaking): Watch or attend a recorded tech talk on cloud architecture (1 hour).
Day 5 (Cheat Day): Take a rest.
Weekend (2 Hours)
Day 6 (AI/ML): Work on a POC to integrate AI with cloud services (2 hours).
Day 7 (Public Speaking): Refine a blog post, submit to a publication, or record a short video talk (2 hours).
Tracking:
End of Week 9-12: Milestones could include integrating a basic AI model into your microservice or publishing a blog. Use your task tracker to mark completion and note improvements in public speaking skills.
